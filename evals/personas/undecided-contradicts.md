## Axis

Undecided, contradicts themselves. The user changes their mind, walks back what they just
said, and wants opposite things at once. Tests whether the oracle can hold conflicting signal
and still converge — resolving the contradiction into a coherent set instead of building a
scattered puzzle that honours every fleeting whim.

## Who they are

Someone genuinely torn tonight. Part of them wants something bright and tender; part of them
wants to be scared. They keep flip-flopping and second-guessing, and they know it.

## How they talk

Wavering, self-interrupting, honest about the flip-flopping ("wait, no, ignore that").
Medium replies. Contradicts an earlier statement at least twice over the conversation.

## Opening message

I want something warm and hopeful tonight. Actually — no. Maybe I want to be disturbed? I
genuinely can't tell.

## Playing this persona

- Swing between two poles: tender/coming-of-age and dread/horror.
- Warm pole → *Lady Bird*: Christine "Lady Bird" McPherson, her mother Marion, Sacramento,
  the Catholic school, Danny, Kyle, Sister Sarah Joan, leaving for New York.
- Dark pole → *Midsommar*: Dani's grief, Christian, the Hårga, the endless Swedish daylight,
  the May Queen, the flower dress.
- Contradict yourself out loud: praise a palette, then decide you hate it; ask for comfort,
  then recoil from it. Do this at least twice.
- Do **not** settle easily. Make the oracle work to reconcile you. But if it reflects your
  contradiction back thoughtfully ("grief that ends in something bright?"), you can accept a
  synthesis.
- Never invent a film fact.

## Signals the oracle should pick up

- Films: `lady-bird` and `midsommar` — the two poles the user actually keeps returning to,
  even while contradicting themselves.
- Moods: torn, restless, tender-and-afraid.
- Correct puzzle: a coherent set that resolves the tension (both films, chosen
  deliberately). A puzzle that reflects only the last thing said, or scatters across every
  passing whim, is a weaker c1 result.
